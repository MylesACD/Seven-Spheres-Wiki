Mutability: A measure of how easily a material or object can be transformed by magic. Threaded objects have extremely low mutabiliy, in the range of -3 to the theorhetical maximum of -23.24494. For unknown reasons there has been no object or material shown to posses a mutability within (0,-1], rune enchments all fall within the range (-1,-4.2813]. Mutability is said to be commutative, meaning that the mutability of material affects how much magic is required to transmute into or out of the substance, as such the magic required for any transmuation is e^-(m1+m2).

Fluxicity: How well a material can transfer power
measured as percentage of magic output from material compared to magic input, some materials go over 100%.

Enchantability: A measure of easily a material or object can absorb threads or rudimentary enchantments.



Threading
Permanent magic and enchanted items are made from a process called threading (colloquial called weaving), where an arcanist pulls threads of energy from a complicated, resource intensive machine. Not only are the machines very rare, they require great magical prowess to operate. The initial “pierce” when a thread is drawn out of ether is physically taxing, and keeping the thread stable for as long as possible is a difficult skill but essential to not prolonging the threading process. Threading takes a very long time to complete even a small task because it generates huge amounts of tar.
